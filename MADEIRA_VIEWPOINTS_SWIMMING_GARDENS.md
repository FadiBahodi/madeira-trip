#### 1. Cabo Girão Skywalk
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **GPS** | 32.656658, -17.004539 |
| **Altitude** | 580 m (1,932 ft) — highest sea cliff in Europe |
| **What you see** | Glass-bottom skywalk over sheer cliff drop to ocean; fajãs (farming terraces) at the base; Funchal coastline east; Câmara de Lobos west |
| **Best time** | Early morning (08:30-10:00) or late afternoon (after 16:00). Tour buses flood 10:00-14:00 |
| **Parking** | Free car park, sizeable but fills fast with tour buses. Arrive early |
| **Entry fee** | ~2-5 EUR (has changed recently; was free, then 2 EUR, reports of 5 EUR in 2026). Toilets 0.50 EUR |
| **Hours** | Daily 08:30-20:00 (summer), 08:30-19:00 (winter) |
| **Difficulty** | None — wheelchair accessible, paved path from car park |
| **Facilities** | Large cafe, souvenir shop, public toilets, cable car down to fajãs |
| **Worth-it** | 10/10 — non-negotiable. Even if you hate heights, the views are extraordinary |
| **Notes** | Cable car descends to Fajã dos Padres area (~5 min ride). Can also do boat tour from Câmara de Lobos to see cliffs from sea level |

#### 2. Eira do Serrado (Nun's Valley)
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **GPS** | 32.710430, -16.965680 |
| **Altitude** | 1,095 m |
| **What you see** | Crater-like Curral das Freiras (Valley of the Nuns) surrounded by towering peaks; village at bottom. Near-360-degree mountain panorama |
| **Best time** | Morning for clarity; clouds often roll in by afternoon. Avoid midday haze |
| **Parking** | Large, easy parking at Estalagem da Eira do Serrado hotel |
| **Entry fee** | Free |
| **Difficulty** | Easy-moderate. ~15 min walk uphill from hotel to viewpoint platform |
| **Facilities** | Restaurant at hotel, cafe |
| **Worth-it** | 9/10 — the "postcard view" of Madeira's interior. Only 25 min from Funchal |
| **Notes** | Can combine with drive down into Curral das Freiras village for chestnuts and poncha. The nuns fled here from pirates in the 15th century — the valley was so remote it was considered impregnable |

#### 3. Pico do Arieiro (Areeiro)
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **GPS** | 32.740251, -16.942484 (Pico Cidrão nearby viewpoint) |
| **Altitude** | 1,818 m — 3rd highest peak on Madeira |
| **What you see** | Above the clouds. 360-degree mountain panorama: Pico Ruivo (1,862 m), Pico das Torres (1,851 m), volcanic ridges, sea of clouds below, ocean in distance |
| **Best time** | SUNRISE — arrive 1h before. Pink/orange sky 15 min before sunrise is the magic moment. Sunset also spectacular. Midday often clouded in |
| **Parking** | P1 (summit, 4 EUR/h), P2 (lower, 2 EUR/h, 15-min walk up). Sunrise = easy parking. Midday = can be full |
| **Entry fee** | Free (parking is paid) |
| **Difficulty** | 5-min walk from P1 to main viewpoint (Miradouro do Juncal). Secondary viewpoint Miradouro do Ninho da Manta is 1.2 km walk |
| **Facilities** | Small cafe/bar at summit (limited hours), toilets |
| **Worth-it** | 10/10 — life-list sunrise. Dress WARMLY, temperatures can be near freezing at dawn even in March |
| **Notes** | Starting point for PR1 hike to Pico Ruivo (strenuous, 7 km one way). In March, snow/ice possible at summit. Check weather before driving up. Road can close in bad weather |

## Part 2: SWIMMING SPOTS

### March Conditions Note
**Water temperature in March: 17-19 C (63-66 F)** — the coldest month of the year for ocean water. Swimmable but bracing. Natural pools are roughly same temp as ocean (may warm slightly in sun). Wetsuit recommended for extended swimming. South coast tends slightly warmer and calmer than north.

| **Entry fee** | 15 EUR (adults) / Free under 14 with adult |
| **Hours** | Apr-Sep: 09:00-19:00 / Oct-Mar: 09:00-18:00. Daily except Dec 25 |
| **Ticket includes** | Garden + Monte Palace Museum (3 floors: minerals, precious stones, modern art) + Moscatel wine tasting at Bluehouse Cafe |
| **Highlights** | 70,000 m2. Koi ponds, Japanese-style gardens, azulejo tile panels (Portuguese history in tiles), African sculpture collection, enormous variety of tropical/subtropical plants, cycads, tree ferns. Stunning views over Funchal harbor |
| **Access** | Funchal Cable Car from Old Town (currently closed for maintenance until Apr 2026 — shuttle service available instead) OR taxi/car to Monte. Can walk DOWN through Old Monte Gardens after (free) |
| **Time needed** | 2-3 hours minimum |
| **Worth-it** | 10/10 — Madeira's #1 garden. The azulejo panels alone are worth the visit. Combine with Monte toboggan ride down |
| **March notes** | Beautiful in March — subtropical plants in bloom, fewer crowds than summer |

### 4. Jardim do Mar (Village-Garden)
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **GPS** | ~32.740, -17.215 (southwest coast, Calheta municipality) |
| **Type** | Not a formal garden — an entire car-free village nicknamed "Garden of the Sea" |
| **Entry fee** | Free (it's a village) |
| **What to expect** | Picturesque narrow streets with flowers everywhere. Subtropical vegetation. Three pebble beaches (Portinho, Enseada, Ponta Jardim). Famous European surf spot. Ocean-front walking promenade with playground |
| **Swimming** | Concrete boat ramp access to sea. Lots of waves — NOT ideal for swimming. Surfers only |
| **Access** | Drive or PR20 hike from Prazeres |
| **Worth-it** | 7/10 — charming village stroll. Combine with Calheta beach visit nearby. Don't go expecting a formal garden; it's a village with flowers |

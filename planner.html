Madeira in 60 Seconds

Never been? Here's everything you need to understand before reading the day plans.

🛣

What's a Levada?

Irrigation channels built centuries ago to carry water from the wet north to the dry south. They carved flat paths along mountainsides that are now Madeira's signature hiking trails — flat, scenic, following water through tunnels and forests. Most famous hikes follow levadas.

🥾

What's a PR Trail?

PR = Pequena Rota (Short Route). Madeira's official numbered hiking trails (PR1 through PR50+). PR trails require a reservation (€4.50 each) booked on SIMplifica. Book the night before. Screenshot the QR code — there's no signal at trailheads.

🏔

Island Geography

South coast (Funchal) = sunny, urban, your base. North coast = wild, green, rainier. Peaks (1800m) = above the clouds, freezing at dawn. West (Rabaçal, Porto Moniz) = waterfalls, forests, lava pools. The island is small — nothing is more than 1.5h drive from Funchal.

⛅

Weather Strategy

Madeira has multiple microclimates. It can be foggy at the peaks and sunny on the coast simultaneously. Check webcammadeira.com every morning. Clear peaks = do summits. Cloudy peaks = do coast or forest. March: 17-20°C coast, 3-8°C peaks.

🚗

Driving

You need a rental car. Mountain roads with narrow tunnels, hairpin turns, and stunning views. Get automatic transmission — Madeira's 25-30% hills will punish a manual. March: €20-35/day all-in. Local companies are cheaper, include full coverage, and require no deposit. See Renting a Car below.

🍷

Poncha + Espetada

Two things you can't get anywhere else. Poncha: rum + honey + citrus, deceptively strong (~50% ABV masked by sweetness). Always drink fresh, NEVER bottled. Espetada: beef on laurel-wood skewers hung vertically. THE Madeira food experience. Designate a driver for poncha nights.

Renting a Car

You need one. Buses don't reach trailheads. March = low season = best prices of the year.

Always book automatic transmission. Madeira has 25-30% gradient hills, constant hairpin turns, and hill starts at traffic lights. The €5-10/day premium over manual is worth every cent. Automatics sell out first — book early.
The play: Book a compact automatic with full coverage (SCDW + windscreen/tyre waiver) from a local company. €100-180 total for 5 days, no deposit. They meet you at the airport arrivals hall with the car and paperwork — give them your flight number. Compare on DiscoverCars or CarRentalsMadeira.

5-Day Pricing (March, Compact Auto)

  • Local + full coverage: €100-180 total
  • International + full coverage: €175-325 total
  • Aggregator deal: from €75 (check insurance fine print)
  • Manual discount: €25-50 less (not recommended)
  • Airport delivery fee (local): €20-40 (some include it)
  • Child seat / GPS: €5-8/day each (use your phone for GPS)

Insurance — What You Actually Need

  • SCDW (Super CDW) = zero excess. Your damage liability drops to €0. Essential on Madeira's narrow roads
  • WDW (Windscreen + Tyre) = covers glass, mirrors, wheels. Rockfall and tight roads are real risks here
  • "Full coverage" = SCDW + WDW + theft. Most local companies include this for free
  • Credit card insurance: Not worth it. You pay €2,000+ upfront for damage, then fight your card company for months. Just get full coverage for €50-75

Scam Warnings

  • Goldcar: Worst-reviewed at FNC. Pressure upgrades ("your car isn't powerful enough"), aggressive upselling, damage claims weeks after return. TripAdvisor consensus: avoid
  • Enterprise: Some reports of forced insurance upselling and disputed damage
  • Protect yourself: Take date-stamped video of every panel, roof, wheels, interior at pickup AND return. Get staff in the video acknowledging the car's condition. This defeats 100% of fake damage claims

Pickup & Return

  • Local companies: Meet you at arrivals hall with car + paperwork. Give them your flight number when booking so they track delays
  • Return: Drive to rental area across from terminal. Top up fuel first
  • Nearest fuel station: Repsol on R. do Cano, 4 min from airport, open until 22:00
  • Fuel: "Gasoleo" = diesel, "Gasolina 95" = petrol. Check which your car takes. Wrong fuel = not covered by any insurance
  • Hotel drop-off: Many local companies offer this — ask when booking

Driving in Madeira — Honest Assessment

It's not as scary as the internet says. ~70% of your driving is modern highways and well-lit tunnels. Real quote: "Only people who had a scare write about it online — everyone who had no problems doesn't." You will be fine with a compact automatic and common sense.

What Car to Book

  • Get a Peugeot 208 or VW Polo automatic. The sweet spot: enough power for 11%+ gradients, small enough for narrow roads
  • NOT a Fiat 500 / Toyota Aygo — too underpowered. One traveler "almost burned the transmission in the first half hour"
  • NOT an SUV — all roads are paved. The wider body is a liability on narrow streets
  • Engine matters: minimum 1.0L turbo or 1.2L. You need power on the steep climbs

Road by Road

  • VR1 (airport → Funchal): Modern dual carriageway, tunnels, 25 min. Easy. Your arrival drive will be in daylight (sunset Mar 10 = 7:11 PM)
  • ER103 to Pico do Areeiro: Steep hairpins, 11% gradient, but fully paved. Go slow. Restaurant + parking at summit
  • ER110 to Rabaçal/25 Fontes: Easy road across Paul da Serra plateau. Arrive by 9 AM for parking. Check fog forecast
  • Porto Moniz: Use the NEW road (VE2/tunnels). Skip the old ER101 Antiga — rockfall, cliff edges, "drive at your own risk"
  • Paul da Serra plateau: Flat and straight, but 240+ foggy days/year. If fog forecast above 1,000m, postpone
  • Funchal city: Park in a garage (Almirante Reis €10-12/day). Don't drive into Old Town. Walk or use Bolt

Real Hazards

  • Locals drive fast and won't slow down when you merge onto VR1. Pull over on mountain roads to let them pass — don't feel pressured
  • Rockfall: Real risk on old roads after rain. Stick to new routes with tunnels and netting
  • Fog at altitude: Can roll in fast. Check webcams before driving above 1,000m
  • GPS in tunnels: Loses signal. Some tunnels have roundabouts and exits inside them. Stay alert
  • No Uber. Bolt works in Funchal but only ~300 vehicles island-wide. Unreliable outside the city

If You're Nervous

  • Hybrid approach: Rent a car for the easy routes (east coast, Câmara de Lobos, Santana) and book a guided 4x4 tour for the west coast/Porto Moniz (~€59/person on GetYourGuide)
  • Avoid at night: Mountain roads after dark. VR1 highway is fine
  • Leave the car at the hotel when exploring Funchal on foot. Only drive it for day trips
  • Take your time. You are on vacation. There is no hurry. Drive slow, stop at viewpoints, let locals pass

Parking

  • Free parking: Reid's, Cliff Bay, Quinta da Casa Branca, Quinta Jardins, Quintinha São João, ARTS IN, Three House, all outside-Funchal hotels
  • Paid (~€15/day): Savoy Palace, Castanheiro, most central hotels
  • No on-site: Barceló Old Town, Sé Boutique, Jaca Hostel (need public garage)
  • Street parking in Funchal center is nearly impossible and never free

Breakfast

  • Outstanding: Quinta Jardins do Lago, Reid's, Castanheiro, Cliff Bay, Quinta da Casa Branca, ARTS IN
  • Good: Barceló, Porto Santa Maria, Quintinha São João, Quinta do Furão
  • Big but meh: Hotel Alto Lido (quantity over quality, bad coffee)
  • Hotel breakfast in Madeira is generally excellent. Take it if included — saves time for early hiking starts

Split Stay?

  • Safest bet: Funchal base all 4-5 nights. Nowhere is more than 90 min drive
  • Best split: 3 nights Funchal + 1 night Calheta/Saccharum (sunset) or Porto Moniz (pools)
  • Closest "escape": Pestana Churchill Bay in Câmara de Lobos — 10 min from Funchal, different vibe
  • Do NOT split into more than 2 bases on a 5-night trip — packing/unpacking eats into short trips

Walkability Tiers

  • Walk everywhere (<10 min): Three House, Barceló, Porto Santa Maria, Hotel Madeira, Castanheiro, Sé Boutique, Hotel Catedral, Jaca Hostel
  • Walkable with effort (10-15 min): The Vine, Pestana CR7, ARTS IN (steep return)
  • Shuttle/taxi recommended (20+ min): Savoy, Quintinha, Reid's, Quinta Jardins, Cliff Bay
  • Transport essential (30+ min): Quinta da Casa Branca, Alto Lido

Rabaçal levadas · Ancient forest · Lava pools · North coast
Full day out
🌊
This day works in most weather. Levada hikes are in forest (sheltered from rain). Fanal forest is actually MORE magical in fog. Only Porto Moniz pools need decent weather. If raining hard, swap pools for Porto Moniz Aquarium (€7).
07:00Drive to RabaçalDrive
60 min from Funchal via VE1 west → VE3 up to Paul da Serra plateau → ER110 to Rabaçal. Park at the upper lot, then walk 20 min downhill on paved road to trailhead (shuttle runs from 10am, but you're early). Arrive by 08:00 — parking fills by 9am and it's a zoo by 10am.

Day 2 Tips

  • HEADLAMP is mandatory for PR6 (800m dark tunnel). Phone flashlight is NOT enough
  • Arrive Rabaçal parking by 08:00. Shuttle doesn't run until 10am so walk the 20-min paved road down to trailhead
  • Do PR6.1 (Risco) first, then PR6 (25 Fontes) — same trailhead, 4-5h combo total
  • Fanal forest: fencing installed in 2026, stay on designated paths. Overcast/foggy days are BEST for photography
  • Longest driving day (~3h total). Funchal → Rabaçal 60min, Rabaçal → Fanal 25min, Fanal → Porto Moniz 28min, Seixal → Funchal 45min
  • Total driving: ~3h. Back in Funchal by ~18:00 if dining north, or ~19:00 if driving back

Friday, March 13 — Deep Green Levada Day

Caldeirão Verde tunnels · Laurisilva forest · Santana · Wine tasting
Adventure day
🌳
Rain? Go anyway. PR9 is in dense forest and tunnels — rain makes it more dramatic, not worse. Only skip if there's a severe weather warning (trails closed island-wide on Mar 3 due to orange alert). The levada waterfalls are at their most powerful after rain.
07:00Drive to QueimadasDrive
60 min from Funchal via VE2/VE4 tunnels north → ER101 to Santana → narrow mountain road up to Queimadas Forest Park. Parking €2/hr, €10 daily cap. Arrive early — this is the longest hike of the trip.

17:30Madeira Wine TastingWine

Day 3 Tips

  • PR9 has MULTIPLE long dark tunnels. Headlamp is non-negotiable (not phone flashlight). Some tunnels are 100m+ with zero light
  • PR9 is 17.4 km round trip — longest hike of the trip. Start at 08:00, bring lunch/snacks + 2L water
  • Optional extension: Caldeirão do Inferno (adds 4.4km round trip to a bigger waterfall in a spray-filled canyon). Only if started before 8am and trail is open (often closed for landslides — check day-of)
  • Save your legs — no PR11 today. Tomorrow is Vereda do Larano (13km), you'll need fresh legs
  • Pereira d'Oliveira's free tastings include wines from the 1850s. Ask for the Malmsey and Verdelho
  • Total driving today: ~2h 05min. Lightest driving day of the trip

Saturday, March 14 — Sea Cliffs + Culture + Farewell Espetada

Vereda do Larano · Monte Palace · Cabo Girão skywalk · Câmara de Lobos · Espetada
Hike + Culture
🌞
If it's raining hard: Skip Larano (exposed coastal cliffs) and swap to a culture morning: Mercado dos Lavradores (opens 7am Sat, closes 2pm) → Fábrica Santo António bakery → Rua de Santa Maria painted doors. Then continue with Monte Palace from 11:00.

Day 4 Tips

  • Vereda do Larano: NOT for people with fear of heights — sections have no guardrails next to sheer drops. But if you're comfortable with exposure, it delivers the best views-to-crowds ratio of any trail
  • Out-and-back from Porto da Cruz means no car logistics. Walk to Boca do Risco and back — you get all the dramatic coastal cliff sections
  • Cable car to Monte CLOSED until Apr 5 — taxi up (~€8, 17 min) or bus #20/#21
  • Monte Palace → toboggan down is the natural flow. Garden first, then sled down the hill
  • Santo Antonio: NO reservations. Saturday is busy — arrive by 6pm or expect 30-45 min wait. It seats 200+ but fills fast
  • Last dinner — go for the espetada. It's THE food experience you can't replicate outside Madeira
  • Buy souvenirs today: Madeira wine, bolo de mel, poncha mix (duty-free also available tomorrow)
  • Total driving today: ~2h 30min. Back in Funchal by ~20:00 after dinner

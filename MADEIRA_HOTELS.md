# Madeira Hotel Guide -- March 10-15, 2026

Last researched: March 2026. Prices are approximate for March 2026 (low season). All Booking.com ratings and review counts verified via web search at time of writing.

---

## SEASON & BOOKING CONTEXT

**March = Low/Shoulder Season**

- Expect hotel prices 20-40% below summer peaks. January-February is the true low, March is shoulder.
- Weather: 16-20C daytime, 10C nights, ~6 hours sunshine/day. South coast (Funchal) is sunnier; north coast is greener but wetter.
- Thin crowds. You will not struggle for availability.
- On average: 3-star hotels ~$120/night, 4-star ~$221/night, 5-star ~$374/night (Booking.com data).
- Fridays tend to be cheapest nights. Book 3-4 weeks out for best rates.

Understanding the layout before choosing:

```
                    FUNCHAL HEIGHTS (Monte, Sao Joao)
                         |  (steep hills, cable car)
                         |
    LIDO/SAO MARTINHO ---+--- FUNCHAL CENTER/SE ---+--- ZONA VELHA/OLD TOWN
    (resort strip,       |   (cathedral, shops,    |   (painted doors, bars,
     promenade, pools)   |    Blandy's Wine Lodge) |    Mercado, waterfront)
                         |                         |
                    WATERFRONT / MARINA / HARBOUR
```

**Key walking distances:**
- Zona Velha to Se Cathedral: ~5-8 min walk
- Se Cathedral to Mercado dos Lavradores: ~3 min walk
- Lido area to Old Town: 25-40 min walk (downhill easier, uphill is steep) or 10 min by bus
- Funchal Heights (Monte) to Old Town: cable car down, or 20+ min steep walk
- Lido promenade runs along the coast west from Funchal center -- flat and scenic

## 1. FUNCHAL CENTER / SE

**What it's like:** The commercial and cultural heart. Se Cathedral, the municipal garden, Blandy's Wine Lodge, shopping streets. Not as "atmospheric" as Old Town but more central to everything. Flat-ish terrain. Restaurants, shops, and cafes everywhere. Bus hub.

**Walking distance:** 3-5 min to Mercado dos Lavradores, 5-8 min to Zona Velha, 15-20 min to Lido. Everything is close.

**Best for:** Walkers who want to be equidistant from all Funchal districts. Mid-range budgets.

## 2. ZONA VELHA / OLD TOWN

**What it's like:** The soul of Funchal. Cobblestone streets, the famous painted doors of Rua de Santa Maria, waterfront bars, the Mercado dos Lavradores. This is where the nightlife is. Buzzy, atmospheric, alive. Can be noisy at night (restaurants/bars until late).

**Walking distance:** 3 min to Mercado, 5-8 min to Se Cathedral, 5 min to marina/waterfront. 25-40 min walk to Lido.

**Best for:** Foodies, nightlife seekers, walkers, culture lovers. Anyone who wants to step out the door and be in the action.

## 3. LIDO / SAO MARTINHO

**What it's like:** The resort strip of Funchal. Runs along the coast west of the center. Where the big luxury hotels cluster. The Lido promenade is a beautiful flat coastal walk. Swimming complexes (Lido Bathing Complex, Ponta Gorda). More residential, quieter at night. This is where you find the big pools, the spas, the ocean-access hotels.

**Walking distance:** 25-40 min walk to Old Town (flat along promenade, steep if cutting through hills). 10 min by bus. 5 min walk to nearest restaurants/shops. Lido Bathing Complex is in the neighborhood.

**Best for:** Resort seekers, luxury travelers, spa lovers, families. People who want a big pool and ocean views to come back to after hiking days.

## 4. FUNCHAL HEIGHTS (Monte, Sao Joao)

**What it's like:** Quiet residential hills above Funchal with panoramic views of the city, bay, and mountains. Cooler, greener, more peaceful. The Monte cable car connects to the city center. Botanical gardens are up here. You feel removed from the bustle but can see all of Funchal below.

**Walking distance:** 20+ min steep walk down to center. Cable car is the practical option. Or a 10-min drive/taxi.

**Best for:** Couples seeking tranquility, garden lovers, people with a rental car, anyone who prioritizes views and quiet over walkability.

## TOP PICKS BY STRATEGY

### "Money is no object"
**Reid's Palace.** History, Michelin dining, cliff-carved pool, Belmond service. Nothing else competes on heritage. March may dip below 300 EUR.

### "Best all-round luxury"
**Savoy Palace.** Best facilities in Funchal (spa, pools, restaurants). Modern, impressive, consistent. Watch for hidden charges.

### "Highest-rated hotel in Funchal"
**Quinta Jardins do Lago.** 9.7/10 on Booking.com, #1 on TripAdvisor. Garden paradise in the heights. From 148 EUR in March.

### "Best boutique experience"
**Three House Hotel.** 9.6/10 with only 13 apartments. Free parking in Old Town. Full kitchen. Intimate, personal, exceptional.

### "Best value in Funchal"
**Castanheiro Boutique Hotel.** Central location, rooftop pool, spa, excellent breakfast, from 99 EUR. The value benchmark.

### "Best budget surprise"
**Quintinha Sao Joao.** A 5-star with free breakfast, free parking, heated pools, and spa FROM 93 EUR in March. Read that again.

### "Best for foodies"
**The Cliff Bay.** Il Gallo d'Oro (2 Michelin stars) on-site, plus ocean access. Or **Porto Santa Maria** for kitchenette + Old Town restaurant access.

### "Best atmosphere"
**Quinta da Casa Branca.** Private tropical wine estate that happens to be a hotel. Book the Manor House suite.

### "Best outside-Funchal base"
**Pestana Churchill Bay, Camara de Lobos.** 9.0/10, from 80 EUR, real village character, 10 min to Funchal. Close enough to be practical, far enough to be different.

### "Best for one night split stay"
**Saccharum (Calheta)** for sunset. **Aqua Natura (Porto Moniz)** for natural pools at sunrise. **Quinta do Furao (Santana)** for north-coast hiking.

### "Best budget play"
**Hotel Alto Lido.** 4-star, from 67 EUR, heated pools, sea views, Lido location. Or **Jaca Hostel** at 25-50 EUR if you just need a clean bed.

## SPLIT STAY VS. FUNCHAL BASE

**For a 5-night trip (Mar 10-15): Funchal base is safest.**

The island is small. Nowhere is more than ~1.5 hours by car. Day trips everywhere are doable. Moving hotels eats into a short trip.

**If you split:**
- 3 nights Funchal + 1 night Calheta (sunset) or Porto Moniz (pools) + 1 night back in Funchal
- Do NOT split into more than 2 bases
- Santana and Sao Vicente are better as day trips

**Best split combo:**
- Funchal base: **Castanheiro** (99-160 EUR) or **Three House** (120-200 EUR)
- One-night: **Saccharum in Calheta** (130-230 EUR, for sunset)
- Alternative one-night: **Pestana Churchill Bay** in Camara de Lobos (80-150 EUR, for village vibe without being far)

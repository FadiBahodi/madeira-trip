# Madeira Restaurant Guide -- By Geographic Area

Quick-reference restaurant lookup organized by where you will actually be each day. Every restaurant below is verified on Google Maps with current ratings. Last researched: March 2026.

**How to use:** Find the area you are in, pick a restaurant, go. Each entry has the essential info: rating, cuisine, price, what to order, and any warnings.

**Price key:** EUR per person for a main course + drink.
- EUR = under 15
- EUR EUR = 15-30
- EUR EUR EUR = 30-60+

---

## 1. FUNCHAL -- ZONA VELHA (Old Town)

*The evening destination. Rua de Santa Maria and the streets around Forte de Sao Tiago. Walk the painted-door street, pick a restaurant, sit down.*

### Armazem do Sal
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars (~1,200 reviews)
- **Cuisine:** Portuguese fusion, seafood, Mediterranean
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Octopus risotto; trout from Ribeiro Frio; soft-shell crab on hummus
- **Warnings:** Michelin-recommended, so reserve ahead. The 6-course prix fixe gets mixed reviews -- better to order a la carte. Set inside a restored medieval salt warehouse with atmospheric stone walls and wooden beams.

### Restaurante Cidade Velha
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars (~1,600+ reviews on aggregated platforms)
- **Cuisine:** Portuguese, Mediterranean, seafood
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Swordfish stuffed with shrimp; tuna steak with fried corn; fresh fish of the day
- **Warnings:** Live music many evenings (good or bad depending on your mood). Located next to Forte de Sao Tiago with sea view from outdoor tables. Book for dinner -- popular with both tourists and locals.

### La ao Fundo
- **Google Maps:** ~4.5 stars (~3,400+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Fusion Portuguese-Asian, seafood
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Tuna tartare (consensus best starter); steak (described as "one of the best pieces of steak in Europe"); sea bass with coconut milk
- **Warnings:** Small and intimate -- BOOK in advance or risk no table. Located at R. Portao de Sao Tiago 17, right by the fort. TheFork rating: 9.2/10.

### ComTradicoes
- **Google Maps:** ~4.5 stars (Tripadvisor top 1%)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Portuguese (Alentejo + Algarve specialties)
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Fish dishes (locally oriented menu); ask the staff for daily specials -- they give genuine recommendations without pressure
- **Warnings:** RESERVE or face long waits. Bright, welcoming interior in the Old Town bohemian quarter. Staff very attentive with check-ins.

### Gaviao Novo
- **Google Maps:** 4.3 stars
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, Portuguese grill
- **Price:** EUR EUR -- EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Grilled limpets (lapas); fresh grilled fish (choose from daily selection); steak
- **Warnings:** Family-run. Seafood and fish are excellent; AVOID the pasta dishes (carbonara and risotto get terrible reviews). Stick to what they do well: grilled fish and shellfish. On Rua de Santa Maria. Not the cheapest, but the fish quality justifies it. TheFork: 9.2/10.

### Taberna Ruel
- **Google Maps:** ~4.3 stars (~6,300+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Portuguese, seafood, regional
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Fish soup; octopus tavern-style; fresh regional dishes
- **Warnings:** Mixed service reviews -- some nights excellent, some nights long waits and disorganized. The wine cellar is outstanding (large Portuguese wine selection). The building won a heritage restoration award. Located at R. de Santa Maria 119. TheFork: 9.2/10. Reserve for dinner.

### Informal
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~3,400 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Modern Portuguese with East Asian fusion (ramen, yakisoba, tempura alongside Portuguese dishes)
- **Price:** EUR EUR (dinner for two ~EUR 80)
- **Must-order:** Octopus (consensus best dish); veal chop with mushroom risotto; fish fillet; ramen (surprisingly good)
- **Warnings:** NO RESERVATIONS -- this is the biggest issue. Queues form before opening, especially for dinner. Arrive before 6pm to secure a table. Located at R. dos Murcas 39. Modern take on Portuguese food with Asian influences. If you can get a table, the food justifies the wait.
- **Google Maps search:** "Restaurante Informal Funchal Madeira"

### Já Fui Jaquet
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (TripAdvisor #70 in Funchal)
- **Cuisine:** Mediterranean, Portuguese contemporary
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Ask for daily specials; seafood dishes
- **Warnings:** Located at Rua de Santa Maria 5, right at the entrance to the Old Town painted-door street. A new take on a classic Funchal name. Well-reviewed, stylish interior.
- **Google Maps search:** "Já Fui Jaquet Funchal Madeira"

### Basmati Indian & Nepali Restaurant
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars
- **Cuisine:** Indian, Nepalese, vegetarian-friendly
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Butter chicken; any curry; vegetarian options (best vegetarian selection in Funchal)
- **Warnings:** Perfect for when you need a break from Portuguese food. Generous portions, lunchtime specials are excellent value. Located at Largo do Corpo Santo 15 in the quiet part of Zona Velha. TheFork food rating: 8.9/10.

## 2. FUNCHAL -- CENTER

*Lunch spots for when you are exploring the city, visiting the market, or between activities.*

### Tipografia
- **Google Maps:** 4.6 stars (~550 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Mediterranean, Portuguese-Italian fusion
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Set lunch menu (changes daily, 2 courses + water + coffee for a fixed price -- excellent value); revisited espada com banana with banana puree
- **Warnings:** Inside Castanheiro Boutique Hotel courtyard. Beautiful patio setting. Lunch is a set menu with limited choice, but the quality is high. Imaginative food, beautifully presented. Good wine selection.

### AKUA by Chef Julio Pereira
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars
- **Cuisine:** Contemporary seafood, Portuguese
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Tuna tartare; cod tacos; seafood rice
- **Warnings:** BOOK for lunch -- it gets busy, especially Sundays. Open kitchen where you can watch chefs work. Located on Rua dos Murcas 6, a calm side street. Minimalist decor. This is the lunch spot that food-obsessed locals recommend.

### Rustikus
- **Google Maps:** 4.9 stars (~180 reviews)
- **Cuisine:** Portuguese home-cooking, daily specials
- **Price:** EUR (6.90 per dish)
- **Must-order:** Whatever the prato do dia is -- menu changes daily with 2 fish and 2 meat options. Everything is fresh and homemade.
- **Warnings:** WEEKDAYS ONLY, noon to 4pm. No dinner. Arrive early or queue -- locals pack this place. Generous portions. This is the best-value lunch in Funchal, period.

### Restaurante Londres
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Portuguese, regional
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Tuna steak with fried corn; bacalhau; espetada. SKIP the salmon -- order island fish instead.
- **Warnings:** 40+ years in operation. Rua da Carreira, city center. Mostly locals at lunch. Canteen atmosphere -- not romantic, but the food is honest. Portions are enormous and can be shared. Very wallet-friendly. Daily pratos do dia.

### A Tendinha
- **Google Maps:** ~4.3 stars
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, snack bar
- **Price:** EUR (budget)
- **Must-order:** Whatever is on the daily whiteboard -- fish or meat with potatoes and salad
- **Warnings:** CASH ONLY. Menu written on whiteboard, changes daily. No-frills, quintessentially Madeiran. Fresh, simple, cheap. If you want the tourist-free local experience, this is it.

### Snack-Bar Bela 5
- **Google Maps:** 4.8 stars (~TripAdvisor #24 in Funchal)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, traditional Portuguese, snack bar
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Prego no bolo do caco (steak sandwich in Madeiran bread -- the signature); grilled fish of the day; seafood rice; fish soup
- **Warnings:** Lively, quirky, family-friendly atmosphere. Generous portions at very reasonable prices. Service is friendly even when busy. Located at Rua Bela São Tiago 5A. Great for a quick, satisfying lunch after a hike without the formality of a full restaurant.
- **Google Maps search:** "Snack Bar Bela 5 Funchal Madeira"

### Kampo by Chef Julio Pereira
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars
- **Cuisine:** Contemporary European, seasonal, tasting menu
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR (sharing menu EUR 98 for two / 6 dishes)
- **Must-order:** The "To Share" 6-course menu for two (EUR 98) -- inventive, seasonal, not pretentious; individual dishes also available
- **Warnings:** This is the UPSCALE lunch option. Chef Julio Pereira also runs AKUA (above). Inventive, contemporary, seasonal menu that changes frequently. On Rua dos Murcas. Not a post-hike-in-shorts place -- more of a "we showered and want a nice lunch" destination. Book ahead.
- **Google Maps search:** "Kampo Chef Julio Pereira Funchal Madeira"

## 3A. CANIÇAL (Closest to PR8 Trailhead -- 5 min drive)

*If you want to eat immediately after PR8 Sao Lourenco without driving to Machico. Caniçal is the fishing village right next to the trailhead.*

### O Recante
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~1,420 reviews on Restaurant Guru; 4.8 on TripAdvisor)
- **Cuisine:** Modern Portuguese, ocean-inspired seafood
- **Price:** EUR -- EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Garlic prawns; octopus tentacles; tuna (the restaurant's specialty -- Caniçal was the whaling/tuna capital of Madeira); escabeche limpets
- **Warnings:** Chef Eusébio's menu changes with the catch. Prices are among the LOWEST on Madeira for this quality. Modern interior, beautifully presented dishes. 5-minute drive from PR8 trailhead. The best option if you want quality seafood without driving to Machico.
- **Google Maps search:** "O Recante restaurante Caniçal Madeira"

### Restaurante Leone
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars (~440 reviews on Restaurant Guru; TheFork 8.7/10)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, family-style
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada on laurel stick (tender, smoky, garlic-seasoned -- reviewers rave about it); bacalhau; octopus
- **Warnings:** Family-run, warm atmosphere. In Caniçal village center (in front of Santander bank). English menus available. Good portions, fair prices. Not as polished as O Recante but more traditional and homey.
- **Google Maps search:** "Restaurante Leone Caniçal Madeira"

## 3B. MACHICO (East Coast -- 10-15 min from PR8)

*After hiking Vereda da Ponta de Sao Lourenco (PR8) or exploring the east coast. Machico is 10-15 minutes drive from the PR8 trailhead.*

### Restaurante Lily's
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~2,170+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Madeiran, European, seafood
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Fresh fish of the day; desserts ("to die for" per multiple reviews)
- **Warnings:** CLOSED MONDAYS. Open Tue-Sun. Located high above Machico with valley views -- worth the drive up. Best price-to-quality ratio in the area. Staff are multilingual. Reserve for weekend lunch.

### O Grelhador
- **Google Maps:** 4.6 stars (~515 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Grilled fish, espetada, traditional Madeiran
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Lapas (limpets); espetada beef; milho frito; fresh-squeezed orange juice or sangria
- **Warnings:** Eastern side of the Machico valley. Terrace with panoramic views. Fair prices, friendly staff. Garlic bread is standout. Good post-hike option with substantial portions.

### Restaurante & Bar O Casco
- **Google Maps:** 4.2 stars (~2,350 reviews on Restaurant Guru; 4.4 on TripAdvisor with 790 reviews)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, Portuguese
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada; scabbard fish fillet with banana; daily specials
- **Warnings:** Operating 30+ years. Rustic, welcoming. One of the most affordable restaurants in Machico. Generous portions. Great value for money. In Machico town center.

### Restaurante Mercado Velho
- **Google Maps:** ~4.2 stars (~1,450 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Portuguese, seafood, traditional
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Scabbard fish; octopus; chicken in mushroom sauce with Madeiran wine; fish soup
- **Warnings:** Housed in a restored 17th-century building -- charming setting. Fish dishes EUR 10-15. Service can be inconsistent (some visits excellent, others slow). In Machico town center.

### MareAlta -- Peixe & Mariscos
- **Google Maps:** ~4.0 stars (~2,370 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, fish
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Scabbard fish with passion fruit sauce and banana; fresh grilled fish of the day
- **Warnings:** RIGHT ON THE BEACH -- the closest restaurant to the sand in Machico. "Best fish we had on the island" per some reviews. However, service can be slow (30-min waits reported) and staff can seem indifferent. Go for the food and location, not the service.

## 4. NORTH COAST (Porto Moniz, Sao Vicente, Seixal, Porto da Cruz)

*Dinner on Day 2 during a north coast drive. These are spread along the coast -- pick based on where you are when hunger strikes.*

### Restaurante Lavrador -- Sao Vicente
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~2,390 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, interactive dining
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada (meat skewer -- the standout); picanha steak; cook-your-own beef or tuna on hot stone; homemade bolo de mel
- **Warnings:** THE BEST restaurant on the north coast by ratings. Stunning valley views. BOOK AHEAD, especially for dinner and terrace seating. Friendly, attentive service. Reasonable prices for quality.

### Quebramar -- Sao Vicente
- **Google Maps:** 4.2 stars
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, seafood
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Caldo da Romaria (traditional pilgrimage soup of Sao Vicente -- you cannot get this elsewhere); espetada on laurel stick; pork in wine and garlic
- **Warnings:** ROTATING PANORAMIC DINING ROOM -- one of few in the world. Established 1982. Views of the Atlantic and Sao Vicente mountains while the room slowly turns. Non-rotating tables available downstairs. Large portions, low prices. Service can be slow. Worth it for the experience.

### Lounge Bar Clube Naval do Seixal
- **Google Maps:** ~4.0 stars (~1,210 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Madeiran, seafood, casual
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Picado; fresh catch of the day; stick to the Madeiran dishes
- **Warnings:** Right by Seixal's natural swimming pool. Popular with surfers and climbers. AVOID pizza and burgers (frozen/dry per reviews) -- order Portuguese dishes only. Owner Bruno is multilingual and very friendly. The location and vibe are excellent even if food is inconsistent.

### Las Caraibas -- Seixal
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~137 reviews; TripAdvisor 4.7)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, international, fusion
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Fresh seafood dishes; grilled meats; ask about daily specials
- **Warnings:** Relaxed, welcoming atmosphere with scenic views. Live music some evenings. Fresh ingredients and bold flavors. Better food quality than Clube Naval next door, but less of a "scene." Good alternative if you want a proper dinner in Seixal rather than a bar snack.
- **Google Maps search:** "Las Caraibas Seixal Madeira"

### Restaurante Polo Norte -- Porto Moniz
- **Google Maps:** ~4.0 stars
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, grilled meats and fish
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Grilled octopus; homemade chocolate cake with coconut filling; set lunch menu (main + dessert + glass of wine ~EUR 20)
- **Warnings:** Open since 1986. Right at the natural pools (Largo das Piscinas). Terrace with ocean views. Open daily 12:00-22:00, good for both lunch and dinner. Accepts reservations and credit cards. More consistent than the other Porto Moniz options.

### Snack Bar A Pipa -- Porto da Cruz
- **Google Maps:** ~4.5 stars
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, seafood, poncha
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Tuna steak (widely called the best on Madeira); grilled limpets; homemade poncha (among the island's best)
- **Warnings:** CLOSED SUNDAYS AND MONDAYS. No reservations -- arrive around noon to avoid queues. Budget prices with generous portions. A true hidden gem. If your north coast route passes through Porto da Cruz, do not skip this.

## 5. RABAÇAL / PAUL DA SERRA (Post-PR6 Hike Lunch)

*After hiking PR6 (25 Fontes / Risco). This is remote plateau country -- options are very limited. Plan accordingly.*

### Rabaçal Nature Spot Cafe
- **Google Maps:** 4.6 stars (~850 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Cafe, snacks, light meals
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Sandwiches (classic Portuguese style); quiche; brownies; bica (espresso) or galão (latte). The dessert selection is surprisingly wide, including vegan options.
- **Warnings:** This is the ONLY food option near the Rabaçal trailhead. It knows it, but prices remain fair for the location. Large windows with panoramic mountain views. Indoor and outdoor seating. Good for a post-hike snack/light lunch, but NOT a full restaurant meal. If you want a proper sit-down lunch, drive north to Porto Moniz/Seixal/São Vicente (30-40 min) or bring packed food.
- **Google Maps search:** "Rabaçal Nature Spot Cafe Calheta Madeira"

### Alternative: Pack a Picnic
- There is a **picnic park** along the Levada das 25 Fontes trail with tables and water. Many hikers buy supplies in Funchal (Pingo Doce supermarket) the night before and eat at the picnic area. This is arguably the better strategy if you want a full meal, since the cafe is snack-oriented.

## 6. SANTANA / QUEIMADAS AREA (Post-PR9 Hike Lunch)

*After hiking PR9 (Caldeirão Verde) or visiting the traditional houses in Santana. The Santana area has several strong options.*

### Restaurante Calhau de São Jorge
- **Google Maps:** 4.9 stars (~234 reviews; TheFork 9.7/10; TripAdvisor #1 in Santana)
- **Cuisine:** Portuguese with contemporary twist, smoked meats and fish
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Slow-smoked beef brisket; grilled limpets; grilled octopus; crème brûlée de maracujá (passion fruit)
- **Warnings:** THE best restaurant in the Santana area by ratings. Located at São Jorge bathing complex (Calhau de São Jorge) -- 15 min drive west of Santana along the coast. Has a SWIMMING POOL you can use. Waterfront setting surrounded by nature. BOOK AHEAD via TheFork. Worth the short detour from Santana.
- **Google Maps search:** "Restaurante Calhau de São Jorge Santana Madeira"

### Cantinho da Serra
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars (~1,000+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, wood-fired oven
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada (cooked to perfection per reviews); bolo do caco with butter/garlic; polvo à Lagareiro (octopus); bacalhau assado no forno (roasted cod)
- **Warnings:** Family restaurant in Santana town center with rustic ambiance. Wood-fired oven is the real deal. Comes with a tray of appetizers (regional starters). Portions are generous. Views of traditional Madeiran A-frame houses across the street. Reasonable prices. Reliable post-hike option right in town.
- **Google Maps search:** "Cantinho da Serra Santana Madeira"

### Quinta do Furão
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars (~1,300+ reviews)
- **Cuisine:** Madeiran-International fusion, fine dining
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Sautéed black scabbard fillet (EUR 21); oxtail tureen with Madeiran red wine; gratinated Azeitão cheese (EUR 23.50); slow-cooked beef cheek
- **Warnings:** Hotel restaurant BUT open to non-guests. Cliff-top location with SPECTACULAR coastline panoramas from the terrace. This is the splurge option in Santana. Prices higher than other Santana restaurants but justified by the setting and food quality. Reserve for terrace seating.
- **Google Maps search:** "Quinta do Furão restaurante Santana Madeira"

### Serra e Mar
- **Google Maps:** ~4.5 stars (TripAdvisor 4.5)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Portuguese, regional
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Fresh fish of the day; regional meat dishes; ask for daily specials
- **Warnings:** Described as a "hidden gem" with outstanding food and service. Less well-known than Cantinho da Serra or Quinta do Furão, which means easier to get a table. Good fallback if others are full.
- **Google Maps search:** "Serra e Mar restaurante Santana Madeira"

## 7. CAMARA DE LOBOS / ESTREITO DE CAMARA DE LOBOS

*The espetada capital of Madeira. This is where locals go for serious meat on laurel sticks. Two distinct zones: Camara de Lobos (the fishing village by the sea) and Estreito de Camara de Lobos (up in the hills among the vines).*

### Restaurante Santo Antonio -- Estreito
- **Google Maps:** ~4.6 stars (~9,080+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, espetada specialist
- **Price:** EUR EUR (EUR 25-30 per person with wine)
- **Must-order:** Espetada on bay laurel stick (THE signature dish); milho frito; french fries with garlic and oregano
- **Warnings:** THE most famous espetada restaurant on the island. Operating since 1966. NO RESERVATIONS -- first come, first served. But service is fast, so turnover is quick. Drive up through the mountain vineyards is part of the experience. Dinner for two with wine rarely exceeds EUR 30-35. A Madeira pilgrimage.

### O Polar -- Camara de Lobos
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~4,110+ reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Espetada, grilled chicken, simple Madeiran
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada a Madeirense (one skewer often feeds two); garlic bread (claimed best on the island); passion fruit pudding (homemade)
- **Warnings:** CASH ONLY (or Portuguese bank card). No reservations. Expect to wait during peak hours and weekends. Modest appearance but the meat quality is exceptional. Keep it simple: espetada + garlic bread + beer. That is the experience.

### Restaurante Viola -- Estreito
- **Google Maps:** ~4.6 stars (~3,670 reviews on Restaurant Guru; TripAdvisor 4.5)
- **Cuisine:** Traditional Madeiran, espetada, grilled meats
- **Price:** EUR
- **Must-order:** Espetada (you can watch it being cooked on the open grill); dry-aged meats
- **Warnings:** BOOK AHEAD or wait. Always busy. Unbeatable prices for the quality. Eat-with-the-locals atmosphere. Direct view of the open grill is part of the charm.

### Vila da Carne -- Camara de Lobos
- **Google Maps:** 4.4 stars
- **Cuisine:** Meat-focused, espetada, artisanal sausages
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Espeto de carne de Madeira (traditional espetada); artisanal sausage selection; grilled meats
- **Warnings:** More upscale than O Polar or Santo Antonio. Elegant atmosphere with charming views over the port of Camara de Lobos. Good value despite slightly higher prices than the traditional spots. TheFork: 8.9/10.

### Vila do Peixe -- Camara de Lobos
- **Google Maps:** ~4.3 stars (~4,320 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, grilled fish
- **Price:** EUR EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Pick your own fish from the display -- tuna, sea bream, parrot fish, black scabbardfish. Simply grilled with salt and olive oil over open fire embers.
- **Warnings:** This is the FISH counterpart to the meat espetada restaurants. Floor-to-ceiling windows with stunning bay views. RESERVE for weekends. Service can be inconsistent. Some dishes slightly overcooked. Best strategy: pick simple grilled whole fish and let the quality speak.

## 8. CALHETA / WEST COAST

*If overnighting on the west side or doing a day trip to Calheta's sandy beach. Fewer restaurants than Funchal, but some standouts.*

### Restaurante Beira Mar -- Calheta
- **Google Maps:** ~4.6 stars (TripAdvisor 4.6 with 355 reviews, ranked #5 in Calheta)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, Portuguese
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Scabbard fish with passion fruit and banana; garlic bread; grilled shrimp; passion fruit mousse
- **Warnings:** Ocean views from the marina. Generous portions, fair prices. Staff very friendly. On a promontory at Ponta do Este. Reliable, no-surprises option.

### Restaurante O Farolim -- Ponta do Pargo
- **Google Maps:** 4.7 stars (~2,320 reviews on Restaurant Guru)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, Portuguese, grilled fish
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Seafood pasta ("best pasta I've ever had" per reviewers); tuna steak; pork cheeks; red snapper
- **Warnings:** Located in Ponta do Pargo -- the far WESTERN tip of the island. This is NOT in Calheta town; it is a 20-minute drive further west. Worth it if you are doing a full west coast loop. Stunning Atlantic ocean views. GPS directions have been wrong in the past -- use Google Maps, not TripAdvisor GPS.

### Manifattura Di Gelato -- Calheta
- **Google Maps:** 4.6 stars (TripAdvisor 4.5 with ~958 reviews)
- **Cuisine:** Italian (pizza, pasta, gelato), some Portuguese
- **Price:** EUR EUR
- **Must-order:** Thin crispy Italian pizza; homemade gelato (the real draw); garlic bread
- **Warnings:** Harbor views, outdoor seating. Italian-owned, authentic thin-crust pizza and pasta. Also serves osso buco, octopus, beef tartar. Wait times can be long during peak hours. Service sometimes chaotic. Good for a lighter meal or gelato stop after the beach.
